# UI, UX, and Copy

The experience must feel guided, conversational, calm, trustworthy, mobile-first, useful, and Nigerian-market aware. It must not feel scary, manipulative, clinical, robotic, childish, or like a generic SaaS wizard.

Do not ask for contact details before value is shown. Do not ask for BVN, NIN, exact address, payment, policy numbers, documents, or uploads in the POC.

Use human language. Prefer `Your answers may show areas your family should review` over `You are unprotected`. Prefer `Because people depend on your income, life cover may help provide financial support if something happens to you` over robotic gap labels.

Use NEM-aligned burgundy, gold, white, soft neutrals, green for covered/protected, amber for review/gap, and red only for serious warnings. Status must include text, not color alone.

Respect mobile touch targets, visible focus, semantic headings, reduced motion, clear CTAs, and non-blocking support paths.
